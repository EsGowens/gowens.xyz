Svelte 5 has officially arrived, and it completely redefines reactive web engineering. Test

By introducing Runes—explicit signals-based primitives like `$state`, `$derived`, and `$effect`—the Svelte team has discarded historical compiler-magic limits in favor of predictable, modular reactivity.

Props are now simple TypeScript destructuring assignments, slots have been replaced by extremely customizable layout snippets, and components compose cleanly without scope bottlenecks. Svelte 5 yields blazing performance by manipulating the DOM directly without the computational bloat of a virtual DOM.

In this post, we dissect how Svelte 5 handles reactive arrays, manages nested object mutations seamlessly, and explain why developers from React backgrounds are migrating in droves.
